School Shopping List — Store Comparison
Calculating Total Costs Across 3 Stores to Find the Best Deal for Two Shopping Lists
📌 Project Overview
This project solves a real-world budgeting problem: two shoppers, Susan and Tim, each have a school supplies list and need to decide which of three stores — Walmart, Dollar Trap, and Office Repo — offers the best total price for their specific list.
Using Excel, this project calculates the total cost of each person's shopping list at all three stores, then visualizes the comparison with bar charts to clearly recommend the most cost-effective store for each shopper.
🎯 Objectives
Calculate the total cost of Susan's shopping list at each of the 3 stores
Calculate the total cost of Tim's shopping list at each of the 3 stores
Build a bar chart comparing total store costs for Susan
Build a bar chart comparing total store costs for Tim
Recommend the cheapest store for each shopper based on their specific list
📋 The Shopping Lists
Susan's List: 1 USB Stick, 3 Ball Point Pens, 1 TI-35 Calculator, 7 Notebooks, 1 Glue, 2 Tape rolls, 2 Erasers, 10 Pencils, 4 Binders, 1 Box Color Markers, 1 Stapler, 1 Planner, 1 Protractor, 1 Compass, 1 Liquid Paper
Tim's List: 1 USB Stick, 5 Ball Point Pens, 1 TI-35 Calculator, 4 Notebooks, 2 Glue, 2 Tape rolls, 2 Erasers, 10 Pencils, 1 Binder, 1 Box Color Markers, 2 Liquid Paper
Each store (Walmart, Dollar Trap, Office Repo) has different unit prices for every item.
🛠️ Tools & Methods
Microsoft Excel — SUMPRODUCT() to multiply quantity × unit price and sum totals per store
Bar Charts — visual side-by-side comparison of total costs across the 3 stores
Structured data entry — unit prices and quantities organized for easy auditing and updates
🔍 Key Insights
The cheapest store differed depending on the specific mix of items each shopper needed — no single store was the lowest for every individual item
Bulk items with large price gaps (e.g., calculators, USB sticks) had the biggest impact on each shopper's total
This highlights why comparing total list cost matters more than comparing item-by-item — the best store depends on what's actually on your list
